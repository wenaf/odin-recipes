Lazy Fried Rice

I unfortunately have no current images of my amazing fried rice, so in the meantime here is an accurate depiction from catalyststuff on Magnific!

Busy day? Forgot to eat? VERY HUNGRY? Lazy fried rice is excellent, I have no idea how many carbs this is, but generally I consume this when I don't care at all. It is delicious and easy, because everything used I generally have on hand. It's a simple recipe most things can be substituted and many things can be added. I really enjoy the core flavor pallet, but I have made numerous varieties.

Ingredients

  • Frozen dumplings (Can be substituted easily)
  • Rice
  • Eggs
  • Chili Oil
  • Sesame Oil
  • Soy Sauce
  • Onion

Steps

  1. Before even turning on the stove-top begin cooking your rice, if you do not have a rice cooker, get one. The quality of rice doesn't matter too much and when I eat a hot rice bowl I generally use 1:1 water to rice, however for fried rice, I generally use less than 1:1 of water OR use leftover rice.
  2. Add sesame oil to a cold pan and set to medium-high. Sesame oil has a relatively low smoke point so be caucious not to smoke yourself out of the kitchen. Add the frozen dumplings to the hot pan, allow them to pan fry each side, generally allow 2-4 minutes each side flipping when browned and crispy. Once all dumnplings are cooked, remove and add to a small bowl.
  3. Cut or chop the fried dumplings into smaller pieces, and scrable some eggs in the pan. After the eggs are done cooking, add back the dumplings, and now the rice.
  4. Add some chili oil, sesame oil, soy sauce and any other seasonings. Give the rice a good stir and then go-away. Do your dishes and ignore the rice. We want the bottom of the rice amalgamation to get nice and crisp!
  5. After desired crispness is achieved, add to a large bowl and drizzle some sriracha and maybe some green onion if you have it lying around.
